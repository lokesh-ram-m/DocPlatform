# Use Cases for TaskFlow

## Product Manager Scenario
Sarah, a Product Manager, needs to track the progress of ongoing projects within her team. She logs into the TaskFlow application and navigates to the project list page where she can see all current projects at a glance. For each project, she reviews the status and can quickly identify any delays. If a project is falling behind schedule, she utilizes the built-in reporting tools to generate insights on the issues and presents these findings in her weekly team meeting. Additionally, she is able to create new projects directly from the application, defining necessary tasks and assigning them to team members for better organization.

## Business Unit Scenario
David, who heads a business unit, uses TaskFlow to streamline task assignments among his team. He starts his day by checking the task list, reviewing what has been completed and what is outstanding. When a team member approaches him for additional resources, he searches for relevant tasks that need attention and reassigns some of them to others who have bandwidth. He appreciates the easy-to-use interface that allows for quick task modifications without additional meetings. This helps him ensure that the team's workload is balanced and that deadlines are met without stress.

## End User Scenario
Jessica, an end user working on a critical task, accesses TaskFlow to manage her daily work. She logs into the application and navigates to her tasks, where she sees a list of all assigned tasks with their statuses. She clicks on a task to view details, marks it as 'in progress', and adds comments for her reference. Later in the day, she completes the task and promptly updates its status to 'completed'. Jessica also appreciates the application’s notification feature, which alerts her when new tasks are assigned or when deadlines are approaching, helping her stay organized and on track.

## Collaboration Scenario
Mark, a team member, collaborates with his peers using TaskFlow. He starts by reviewing the projects he is involved in and sees updates from his colleagues on task progress. He then uses the application’s commenting feature to ask for clarifications on a task that requires input from another team member. After receiving a timely response, Mark updates the task’s status and continues to provide feedback through the platform. This open line of communication enhances teamwork and productivity, allowing everyone to stay informed and aligned on their objectives.

## Onboarding New Users Scenario
Emily, a new hire, uses TaskFlow to familiarize herself with the projects and tasks her team is working on. Upon logging in for the first time, she accesses a guided tour that helps her understand how to use various features, such as tracking tasks and communicating with her team. She reviews the project list and selects a few tasks that align with her skills for her initial assignments. The application’s user-friendly design makes her onboarding smooth, allowing her to quickly become an effective team member.
