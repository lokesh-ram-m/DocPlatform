# Use Cases for eShopOnWeb

## Use Case 1: Product Manager Managing Inventory
As a Product Manager, Jane logs into the eShopOnWeb application to review the current inventory status. She navigates to the Catalog section where she can see a list of available products. Jane decides to introduce a new catalog item and uses the CreateCatalogItem feature to input details like the product name, price, and description. After successfully adding the item, she verifies its availability by checking the product listing. Jane appreciates how the intuitive interface allows her to manage inventory efficiently, ensuring that her team has the latest products to promote to customers.

## Use Case 2: Business Unit Reviewing Sales Orders
Mark, a member of the Sales team, accesses eShopOnWeb to review recent sales orders. He uses the MyOrders feature to view his sales performance for the week. The application provides him with a detailed list of orders made by customers. Mark clicks on an order to get more information on the products purchased and the status of the shipment. This information helps him communicate effectively with customers regarding their orders. Grateful for the clear layout of the information, Mark can make informed decisions about customer engagement and follow-ups.

## Use Case 3: End User Shopping Online
Jessica, a regular customer, visits the eShopOnWeb platform to purchase items for her home. She uses the search bar to find a specific product and is pleased to see a well-organized display of catalog items. After finding the items she wants, she adds them to her basket using the intuitive interface. Jessica then proceeds to checkout, where she securely logs into her account and completes the purchase. The process is seamless, and she loves receiving a confirmation email almost immediately, reassuring her that her order is being processed.

## Use Case 4: Product Manager Analyzing Market Trends
While planning for the upcoming product launch, Sarah, the Product Manager, uses the eShopOnWeb's API documentation feature to access detailed data regarding catalog items and customer preferences. By navigating the Swagger interface, she retrieves data about popular products and customer demographics. This information enables Sarah to identify market trends and determine which products to focus on in marketing campaigns. She values how the application's analytical capabilities support her strategic decisions, enhancing her confidence in the product launch.

## Use Case 5: End User Managing Account Settings
Mike, an end user, logs into his eShopOnWeb account to update his personal information. He accesses the MyAccount feature to edit his contact details and preferences, which only takes a few clicks. Mike finds options to manage his login credentials and enable two-factor authentication for additional security. After making the changes, he receives a verification email, which he appreciates as an essential security measure. The ease of managing his account settings boosts Mike's trust in the eShopOnWeb application, enhancing his overall shopping experience.
